# Emerging Roles Research Agent

Research framework for discovering AI-adjacent roles not yet captured by O*NET or government occupational taxonomies. Focus: roles with significant adoption/demand signal but <2 years public visibility.

## Mission

Track emerging roles across career clusters that:
- Are gaining traction in AI/tech companies or adjacent industries
- Have significant job postings (100+ on major job boards in past 3 months)
- Are not yet in O*NET taxonomy or have <50 recorded job titles
- Represent genuine new occupations (not rebranding of existing roles)

## Career Clusters & Curated Sources

Each cluster has a dedicated set of sources optimized for surfacing new roles. Update these as sources dry up or new ones emerge.

## Type 2: O*NET Alias Job Titles

These are **real, high-volume job titles that map to existing O*NET codes** but don't appear in O*NET's "Sample Job Titles" list. They're not new roles — they've existed for years — but they're missing from our career pages and score data.

**Why track them:**
- High search volume (e.g. "Social Media Manager" >> "Marketing Specialists")
- Career pages should use the real-world title, not the O*NET bureaucratic title
- Adding them to `ai_resilience_scores.csv` as "Emerging Job Titles" enables the site to surface these pages in relevant searches

**Data file:** `data/emerging_roles/emerging_job_titles.csv`

Schema:
```
onet_code,job_title,notes
```

- `onet_code` — the closest O*NET code (used to inherit score, salary, growth data)
- `job_title` — the real-world title as it appears in job postings
- `notes` — why it maps here; what's different from the O*NET title

**How to find Type 2 titles:**

1. **Job board scan:** Search LinkedIn/Indeed for a broad occupation (e.g. "Marketing") filtered to past 30 days. Note titles that appear 50+ times but aren't in O*NET sample titles.
2. **Google Trends:** Compare O*NET title vs. common variant (e.g. "Marketing Specialist" vs "Content Marketer") — if the variant has higher search volume, it's a candidate.
3. **"People also search for" on Google Jobs:** Start with an O*NET title, note all the related title suggestions.
4. **Validation:** Check if the title appears in O*NET's `Sample Job Titles` field for that code. If not, and it has >500 LinkedIn postings in past 3 months, add it.

**Clusters with high alias density (prioritize these):**
- Marketing & Sales (13-1161.xx) — Social Media Manager, Content Strategist, SEO Specialist, Growth Marketer
- Design & UX (15-1255.xx, 27-1024.xx) — UX Researcher, Product Designer, Motion Designer
- Writing & Content (27-3043.xx) — Copywriter, Content Writer, Technical Content Strategist
- Software (15-1252.xx, 15-1254.xx) — many modern titles not in O*NET

**Integration:** Run `python3 scripts/generate_emerging_job_titles.py` to push titles into the `Emerging Job Titles` column of `data/output/ai_resilience_scores.csv`.

## Research Workflow

### Weekly Discovery Phase (Rotating Clusters)
Research runs on a **6-week rotating schedule** to cover all clusters systematically:
- **Week 1:** AI Engineering & Alignment + Applied AI / Enterprise AI
- **Week 2:** AI Infrastructure & Infra-adjacent + Design & AI
- **Week 3:** Data, Annotation & Labeling + Product Management & AI
- **Week 4:** Marketing & AI Growth + Law & Compliance / AI
- **Weeks 5-6:** Secondary sources, deep-dives on high-signal roles, cross-cluster validation

For each cluster scanned:
1. Scan primary sources (prioritize: company careers pages, job boards, LinkedIn searches)
2. **Capture signals:** Job title, company, posting date, job description snippet, source URL
3. **Deduplication:** Is this a new role or a rename of an existing O*NET occupation?
4. **Validation:** Does it have 100+ postings in past 3 months across major job boards (LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor)?
5. **Triage:** Emerging (strong signal), Experimental (weak signal), Deprecated (not gaining traction)

### Monthly Synthesis
- Cluster findings by role family
- Flag roles ready for O*NET enrichment & scoring pipeline
- Update source performance (which sources had high hit rates?)
- Archive deprecated roles

### Quarterly Deep-Dives
- Pull 3–5 promising roles into full enrichment (task statements, salary data, etc.)
- Run against scoring pipeline
- Prepare for site integration

## Source Validation

Each quarter, audit source health:
- **Dead/archived:** Remove
- **Low hit rate:** Deprioritize or replace
- **High hit rate:** Star and increase check frequency
- **New sources:** Test for 2 weeks before promoting to primary

## How to Extend

**Adding a new cluster:**
1. Define characteristics (what makes a role belong here?)
2. Research & test 8–12 sources
3. Document high-signal roles already visible in those sources
4. Add to this guide with tiers: primary (check weekly), secondary (check monthly), tertiary (quarterly)

**Deprecating a source:**
- Mark as `status: archived` with retirement date
- Replace with a tested alternative
- Document why (dried up, low quality, too noisy, etc.)

## Notes

- **Don't chase noise.** A role posting from one founder doesn't count. Look for patterns across multiple companies, geographies, and time periods.
- **Job titles are messy.** "Prompt Engineer" varies wildly in scope. Cluster by actual responsibilities, not title alone.
- **Keep history.** Even deprecated roles are valuable for trend analysis. Archive don't delete.
- **Feedback loop.** When a discovered role makes it into the scoring pipeline and site, note it. That closes the loop and validates the research process.
